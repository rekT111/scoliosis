Understanding Your Conditions

Three interconnected issues that this program addresses simultaneously

🦴

Reverse C-Type Scoliosis

A single-curve spinal deviation where the spine bends in a C-shape (typically thoracic left or lumbar right, opposite to the common pattern). This program uses Schroth-inspired asymmetric exercises, rotational breathing, and side-specific strengthening to help correct the curve.

⚖️

Lateral Anterior Pelvic Tilt

The pelvis tilts forward (anterior) and drops unevenly side-to-side (lateral), often causing one leg to appear shorter, lower back arch, and hip imbalance. Correction involves stretching tight hip flexors, strengthening glutes and core, and retraining pelvic position.

🤸

Right Shoulder Blade Popping Out

Scapular winging on the right side, often caused by weak serratus anterior, weak lower trapezius, and tight pectoral muscles. This is commonly associated with the scoliosis curve pattern. Correction focuses on serratus activation, scapular stability, and posterior chain strengthening.

Safety Tips & Guidelines

Important rules to follow throughout your 100-day journey

🩺 Get Evaluated First

Before starting, get an X-ray and professional assessment to confirm your exact curve direction, degree, and pelvic tilt measurements. Exercises must match YOUR curve pattern.

🌬️ Breathing is Key

Schroth rotational breathing is the foundation of scoliosis correction. Practice it daily — inhale into the concave side, exhale to activate correction. Never hold your breath during exercises.

🚫 No Pain, No Gain = FALSE

Stop if you feel sharp pain, numbness, tingling, or dizziness. Muscle fatigue and mild soreness are normal; joint pain is not. Modify or skip exercises that aggravate your condition.

📐 Mirror Feedback

Use a mirror for exercises like wall slides, pelvic tilts, and scapular push-ups. Visual feedback helps you maintain proper form and symmetric movement patterns.

⏰ Consistency Over Intensity

20 minutes daily is far more effective than 2 hours once a week. Don't skip days — even on "rest" days, do your breathing and gentle mobility routine.

🪑 Posture All Day

Exercises alone won't fix your posture if you slouch for 10 hours a day. Set hourly reminders to check your sitting posture, pelvic position, and shoulder alignment.

🧘 Foam Roller Daily

Use a foam roller on your thoracic spine daily to improve mobility. Avoid rolling the lumbar spine directly. Roll slowly, 5–10 passes, focusing on tight areas.

📸 Photo Every 2 Weeks

Take front, back, and side photos every 2 weeks in the same lighting and pose. Track changes in shoulder height, scapular position, and pelvic level. Share with your therapist.
